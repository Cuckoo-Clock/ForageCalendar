Don't eat a mushroom unless you are sure of identifictaion, the toxic risks can be severe to fatal, such as liver failure in a matter of days.

Check and double check identification, do a spore print, check all characteristics{stem,cap,base,growth pattern} thorouhjly against multiple sources. Even if you have found the species before, be thorough. Always be aware of potential look-alikes.

Go with a guide, we gain knowledge and experience through social structures.

wash your hands, while you aren't going to be poisoned by touching a mushroom, if toxins can be transfered to your mouth if you are not careful.

Notes on Eating

×

When eating mushrooms found in the wild, always cook them, do not consume them raw.even when certain about identification, begin with eating a tiny portion, each person may have a slightly different reaction.

In Rhode Islad there is a fine handful of delicious edible species that are distincly identifiable, if you are looking to find something for your table, eat those. Outside these forest delights there are also many varieties which have deadly toxic look-alikes, which require extreme diligence in identification, if this webpage is your first introduction to mushroom foraging these mushrooms are not for you. The consequences of indescretion is relatively swift, decidely unpleasant, and potentially fatal.

on our wonderful planet you can eat a lot of different things, but not all are worth eating. Portion-size matters

heck multiple sources, guidebooks are continually being updated and mushroom varieties are being re-classified, theses changes can be contentious and are not all agreed upon.1

Also finding a similar mushroom in another region the local variety may be slightly different. An example from around New England is the Lilac Brown Bolete(binomial)in Maine, guides in other regions of North America list this mushroom as edible. 2
